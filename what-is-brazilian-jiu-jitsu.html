What Is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) is a grappling-based martial art that focuses on leverage, technique, and submissions rather than size and strength.

What Makes BJJ Different?

Unlike striking arts like boxing or Muay Thai, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu focuses on controlling and submitting an opponent on the ground. Practitioners learn how to use positioning, leverage, and timing to overcome larger opponents.

The BJJ Belt System

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu has one of the most respected ranking systems in martial arts:

  • White Belt
  • Blue Belt
  • Purple Belt

Is BJJ Good for Beginners?

Yes. Most gyms welcome beginners and structure classes to accommodate all levels. Technique is emphasized over athleticism, making it accessible for most body types.

Benefits of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

  • Improves strength and conditioning
  • Builds mental resilience
  • Develops real self-defense skills
  • Creates strong community bonds
